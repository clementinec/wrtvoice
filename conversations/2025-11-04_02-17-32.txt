Department of Architecture, The University of Hong Kong Knowles Building, Pokfulam, Hong Kong SAR November 2, 2025 Editor-in-Chief (or Handling Editor) Energy Dear Editors, Please find enclosed our manuscript entitledFuture-weather benchmarking of building con- trollers: energy, peaks and comfort across five climatesfor consideration inEnergy. The paper presents a multi-city, multi-horizon benchmark of machine- and deep-learning HVAC con- trollers under future-weather inputs, jointly evaluatingenergy use,tariff-window peak demand (14:00–20:00), andthermal comfort. The study is controller-agnostic and quantifies grid-relevant impacts (peaks, ramps) alongside standard building metrics, aligning withEnergy’s readership at the building–grid interface. What is new and important.Across five representative climates and three horizons (Historical, Midterm, Longterm), the proposed controllers reduce site EUI by8–11%in cooling-dominant cities and lower tariff-window peak demand by18–30%via pre-cooling. In heating-dominant sites, morning recovery can increase by∼25%, a planning consideration we quantify. Rankings remain stable under a moderate-emissions pathway (RCP4.5 vs. RCP8.5: identical in 57/60 city– horizon cases), indicating pathway-robust guidance. Using representative tariffs (energy$0.08– 0.35/kWh; demand$10–30/kW·month), the Miami reference case converts the reported energy (−6.3 kWh m−2yr−1) and demand-window (−1.8 kW) reductions into roughly$1.0–$3.6 m−2yr−1, illustrating economic significance. Fit forEnergy.The manuscript contributes (i) grid-relevant demand metrics tied to controller policies, (ii) a transparent, reproducible pipeline for future-weather benchmarking, and (iii) deploy- ment guidance that differentiates cooling- vs. heating-dominant sites. We believe it will interest readers working on energy systems integration, building demand flexibility, and planning under climate change. Compliance.This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors approve its submission toEnergy.
